About problems viewing barred-L


Some people will have trouble viewing the barred-L character in their browser. For example, it may show up as a question mark or an empty box. You might be able to fix the problem by selecting View>Character Encoding from your browser's menu bar, and choosing "UTF-8".

If this does not fix the problem, your browser may be an older version that does not support the barred-L. For your convenience, each page on this CD comes in a version which replaces all barred-L characters with slash marks, as is consistent with Dena'ina orthography. To view these pages, click the "no barred-L" button on the top of any page. You will be taken to a page with slash marks instead of barred-Ls. Conversely, on any page containing slash marks, you may click the "barred-L" button to see the page with the barred-L characters.
